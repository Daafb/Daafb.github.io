Research

Thesis

My thesis focused on Gaia Astrometric Exoplanet Candidates (ASOIs) and was supervised by Dr. G.K. Stefansson. As part of the third Gaia Data Release (DR3), 72 exoplanet candidates were identified by the Gaia spacecraft. Spectroscopic follow-up observations (radial velocity, RV) are essential to distinguish between genuine exoplanets and common false positives, particularly spectroscopic binaries.

In this project, I analyzed Gaia DR3 data to identify potential exoplanet candidates beyond the published ASOIs. I also examined the current ASOI list using spectral energy distributions (SEDs) to investigate possible signs of stellar multiplicity. The work contributed to three telescope proposals for state-of-the-art spectroscopic instruments, and three peer-reviewed publications on which I am listed as a co-author.

This thesis marked my first hands-on research experience in astronomy and confirmed my enthusiasm for pursuing a research career in the field.
